Science of Sleep
🧪
Sleep & Testosterone
▾

Testosterone is produced primarily during deep REM sleep. Studies show that reducing sleep from 8 to 5 hours decreases testosterone levels by 10–15% in just one week — equivalent to aging 10–15 years.

The critical window is between 10pm–2am when growth hormone and testosterone pulses are strongest. Missing this window consistently suppresses anabolic hormones and accelerates muscle loss.

💡 8 hours of sleep = up to 30% more testosterone than 5 hours
⚖️
Sleep & Weight Loss
▾

Sleep deprivation disrupts ghrelin (hunger hormone +24%) and leptin (satiety hormone -18%), creating an environment where you crave high-calorie foods and feel less full. People who sleep 6 hours vs 8 hours consume an average of 385 extra calories per day.

Cortisol rises with poor sleep, directly promoting fat storage — especially visceral abdominal fat. This is why you can train hard, eat well, and still struggle to lose fat if sleep is poor.

💡 Poor sleep can reduce fat loss by up to 55% during a diet
💪
Sleep & Muscle Growth
▾

Muscle protein synthesis (MPS) is highest during sleep. Growth hormone (GH) — the primary driver of tissue repair — is secreted in large pulses during stages 3 and 4 of NREM sleep. Without adequate sleep, the gains from your training session are literally lost.

Studies show athletes sleeping 10 hours vs 8 hours improved sprint speed by 9%, reaction time by 15%, and had 20% less daytime fatigue.

💡 Each hour of lost sleep reduces next-day strength by ~1-3%
🧠
Sleep & Cognitive Function
▾

During sleep, the brain's glymphatic system activates and clears metabolic waste products including beta-amyloid (linked to Alzheimer's). Memory consolidation — turning short-term experiences into long-term memories — happens exclusively during sleep.

After 17 hours without sleep, cognitive impairment is equivalent to a blood alcohol level of 0.05%. After 24 hours, it reaches 0.10% — legally drunk in most countries.

💡 One night of 6h sleep reduces focus by 30% — equivalent to 2 nights of no sleep
🌙
Circadian Rhythm & Cortisol
▾

Your circadian rhythm is a 24-hour internal clock regulating nearly every hormonal process. Cortisol naturally peaks at 8am to wake you up and declines through the day. Disrupting this cycle — with late nights, blue light, or irregular schedules — keeps cortisol elevated at night, blocking melatonin and degrading sleep quality.

Getting 10 minutes of sunlight within 1 hour of waking sets your cortisol rhythm and dramatically improves sleep 14–16 hours later. This is one of the highest-leverage habits in human biology.

💡 Morning sunlight is the single most powerful lever for sleep quality

🔒 Privacy & Legal
Data, payments & permissions

Data Collection

All fitness data (weight, nutrition, sleep, steps) is stored locally on your device using localStorage. AnthrosAI does not transmit personal data to external servers except for AI queries sent to Groq's API (anonymized) and payment processing via Stripe.

Stripe Payments

Payment processing is handled by Stripe, Inc. We never store your card details. Stripe may collect device data and usage analytics as per their privacy policy at stripe.com/privacy. Your subscription is governed by Stripe's terms of service.

Google AdSense

The free tier of AnthrosAI may display ads served by Google AdSense. Google may use cookies to serve ads based on your prior visits. You can opt out at google.com/settings/ads. All ads comply with Google's Publisher Policies.

Groq AI

AI coaching responses are processed by Groq, Inc. Messages sent to the AI Coach are transmitted to Groq's API. Do not include sensitive personal information in AI chat messages. Groq's privacy policy applies at groq.com/privacy.

Apple Health / HealthKit

Step counting and health integrations require explicit user permission. Health data is only read, never uploaded. All HealthKit data remains on-device per Apple's guidelines.

Your Rights

You can delete all local data at any time by using the "Log Out" option which clears localStorage. For data deletion requests or privacy inquiries, contact support.

Cookies

This app uses localStorage (not cookies) for session persistence. No tracking cookies are set.
